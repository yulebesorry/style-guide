Blue — #007bff

The dominant display color. Used for all major headings, hero type, card borders, and nav outlines. On a white background it reads as the primary brand hue — energetic and direct.

Orange — #ff3f05

The hot accent. Category labels, decorative dots, CTA elements, and the "About" link in the nav. One active hit per section — never compete with blue.

Lime — #bdfc00

The pop detail. Used inside the record circle on the homepage, callout borders, and active/featured states. Appears rarely — maximum 1–2 times per page.

White — #fdfdfd

The page background. Near-white, not pure — gives the blue and orange room to breathe. All card and section backgrounds sit on this.

01

Rounded cards, hard grid

Cards use 16–20px border-radius. The roundness softens the geometry without losing the grid structure underneath. Everything still aligns to columns.

02

Blue borders everywhere

Card outlines, section dividers, the sidebar, and the nav all use #007bff at 1.5px. The border IS the design system — it unifies every surface.

03

White breathes

The white background is not passive. Space between cards and sections is generous — the content floats, it doesn't stack. If it feels dense, add space.

04

Dots as punctuation

Small orange circles appear at nav logos, section eyebrows, card labels, and button interiors. They're not decorative — they're typographic punctuation in the visual language.

05

VOICE &
TONE

Confident, not stiff

I know what I'm doing and I'm interested in showing you — but I'm not going to oversell it. The work speaks. I just set it up.

Casual, not sloppy

Lowercase is a choice. So is the comma after "pizza." The tone is conversational but the typography is precise — those two things coexist.

Specific over vague

"enthusiastic designer based in old foggy San Francisco" — not "creative professional." Place, attitude, and texture beat generic descriptors every time.

Playful when it fits

@yulebesorry. The handle is the whole bit. Humor lives in details — a project name, a footer link, a self-aware caption. Not forced.

"enthusiastic designer based in old foggy San Francisco — I enjoy building for the web but love creating objects for the real world too."
"Creative and versatile design professional with a passion for innovative solutions and a proven track record of delivering impactful visual experiences."
"A solid foundation in visual design, typography, user experience & type design. Always looking for high-spirited folks to collaborate with."
"I am a highly skilled designer with expertise in multiple design disciplines seeking collaborative opportunities with forward-thinking organizations."

Writing Checklist

✓ Lowercase unless display type

✓ Place-specific, not generic

✓ Concrete nouns over abstractions

✓ Texture in the language

✗ Never: "passionate about design"

✗ Never: "results-driven creative"
